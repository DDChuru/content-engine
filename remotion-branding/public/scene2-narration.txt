Every incident starts right here — and you're never on your own. Meet e-wiser, your guide. It asks one simple question: what happened? Injury. Someone was physically hurt on duty. e-wiser captures the detail, and watches the law: only when it's serious — more than thirteen days off, an amputation, or worse — does it raise a Section twenty-four report to the Department of Labour, with the deadline already set. A personal health event. Someone took ill on site, but the work didn't cause it — a fainting spell, chest pains. Still recorded, still investigated — just not a compensation claim, unless the work made it worse. Property, or machine damage. Something broke, and nobody was hurt. No injury claim — but e-wiser logs it anyway, because today's damage is tomorrow's injury. And a near miss — the close call that didn't land. The cheapest lesson you'll ever get. Capture it, and it never becomes the real thing. So whatever happened, e-wiser records every single incident — and quietly flags the few that the law sends to the Department of Labour. No safety degree required. From there, it routes itself: reported, investigated, actioned by the area manager, and closed by the safety team. The right person at every step. Nothing slips through. That is incident management — the e-wiser way.
